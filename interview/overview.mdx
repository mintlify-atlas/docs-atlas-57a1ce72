## Interview Process

### Typical Interview Rounds

1. **Phone Screen** - Basic technical questions and culture fit
2. **Technical Assessment** - Online coding test or take-home assignment
3. **On-site/Virtual Interview** - Multiple rounds covering:
   - Coding problems
   - System design (for senior roles)
   - Behavioral questions
   - Technical deep-dive

### What Companies Look For

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Problem-Solving Skills">

  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Technical Knowledge">

  <Accordion title="Communication Skills">

## Study Strategies

### Effective Learning Techniques

**Active Recall**: Test yourself regularly rather than just re-reading notes

**Spaced Repetition**: Review topics at increasing intervals to strengthen memory

**Practice Implementation**: Write code by hand and on a whiteboard, not just on computer

**Explain to Others**: Teaching concepts to others helps solidify your understanding

### Time Management

<Warning>
Don't try to cover everything at once. Focus on depth in key areas rather than breadth across all topics.
</Warning>

- **Daily Practice**: 2-3 hours of focused study
- **Weekly Review**: Revisit weak areas and difficult concepts
- **Mock Interviews**: At least 1-2 per week in final month

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Note>
These mistakes can cost you the interview, even if you're technically competent.
</Note>

1. **Jumping to Code Too Quickly** - Always clarify requirements first
2. **Not Testing Your Code** - Walk through examples and edge cases
3. **Ignoring Optimization** - Discuss time/space complexity and potential improvements
4. **Poor Communication** - Stay engaged and explain your thinking process
5. **Giving Up Too Easily** - Ask for hints and keep trying different approaches

## Additional Tips

### For the Interview Day

1. **Prepare Your Environment** - Ensure good internet, quiet space, working webcam
2. **Have Materials Ready** - Paper, pen, water, and your resume
3. **Arrive Early** - Join 5-10 minutes early for virtual interviews
4. **Stay Calm** - Take deep breaths if you get stuck
5. **Ask Questions** - Always prepare questions for your interviewer

### After the Interview

- Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
- Reflect on what went well and what to improve
- Keep practicing regardless of the outcome
- Track your progress and adjust your study plan

<Check>
Remember: Interview preparation is a marathon, not a sprint. Consistent, focused practice over time yields the best results.
</Check>
